FIRE AT SCHOOL

CAUSES

FAULTY ELECTRICAL EQUIPMENT

Tends to be caused by defective wiring, overloaded sockets and plug that is way more old and malfunctioning that lead to sparks that may start a fire.

NEGLIGENCE

Opportunities for fires to start at work due to negligence and lack of care, whereas an attempt to get a task done quicker, short cuts or procedures to get the job done faster which can cause major fire and safety risk.

LACK OF STAFF TRAINING

Unknowingly Blocking the vents of electrical equipment that requires air to keep itself cool.

LACK OF RESOURCES

Without proper equipment a fire can quickly reach a dangerous stage before you are even aware of it.

BEFORE

PLAN AHEAD

Plan ahead for prevention in case of emergency.

ENSURE SAFETY PRECAUTIONS

Make sure all safety recommendations at your facility are being followed.

PROMINENTLY DISPLAY SCHOOL BLUEPRINTS

Displaying school blueprints will help staff and students find the closest exit and help firefighters navigate the school in case of an emergency.

PRACTICE DRILLS AND INSTRUCTION

Can help simulate for real-life emergencies.

TEACHING FIRE EDUCATION

Remind students that drills should be practiced. Teach them basics of “stop, drop, and roll”, getting low to the floor, and touching the door handle before opening doors.

DURING

STUDENTS AND STAFF SHOULD EVACUATE THE BUILDING QUICKLY

The best method is to stay calm and follow the emergency procedures.

ONCE OUTSIDE, STUDENTS SHOULD MOVE AWAY FROM THE BUILDING

Make sure all safety recommendations at your facility are being followed.

TEACHERS SHOULD ACCOUNT FOR ALL STUDENTS

Displaying school blueprints will help staff and students find the closest exit and help firefighters navigate the school in case of an emergency.

FIREFIGHTERS SHOULD BE NOTIFIED OF THE FIRE’S LOCATION

Can help simulate for real-life emergencies.

STAY CALM

Staying calm during a school fire is crucial as it allows for clear thinking, swift action, and the safety of oneself and others.

AFTER

STAY OUT OF FIRE DAMAGE

Avoid being near any burning or burned structures.

NOTIFY YOUR FAMILY ABOUT YOUR SITUATION

Contact any individual in the family to know your current statues.

FIND A PLACE TO STAY

Take shelter somewhere safe with, supported with staff and availability of communication.

GATHER EVIDENCE

Take a picture of the damages and take a video clips in a safety place.

DO NOT ENTER THE BUILDING

Fire officers will need to check the status of the building.
